A philosophy
of restraint.

There is a moment, just after the cherry is picked, when the coffee stops belonging entirely to us. It passes into the care of something older and less visible — communities of wild yeasts and bacteria that have lived in our soil, our air, and our forest canopy since long before the first Geisha tree took root at Misty Mountain.

They live in the fruit itself. They live in the hands that harvest it. They live, quietly, in the walls and tanks of our own mill, having settled there season after season until they became, in their own way, residents of the estate. We do not introduce them. We do not need to. They are already home.

This is the heart of what we call Wildynamics — our belief that the finest expression of coffee is not engineered, but revealed.

The unseen
made visible.

Yeasts move first, feeding on the sugars of the mucilage. Lactic bacteria follow as the environment turns to their liking, then acetic bacteria at the margins, where air reaches the fruit. Each population makes the conditions for the next, then recedes.

The cup is the record of that succession.

The Spirit of Longboard

Distilled from Misty Mountain Geisha itself, the Spirit of Longboard is presented each year to the bidder who places the highest single bid across the auction.

One bottle. One recipient. A gesture toward the philosophy that produced the vintage — that what emerges from wild fermentation may become something more than coffee.

FORMAT

Two phases. Silent bidding through the opening window, then a three-minute countdown that resets with every new bid. Fourteen lots, sold in sequence.

REGISTERING TO BID

Bidding is through M-Cultivo, which handles accounts and approval. Approval is manual and can take up to 48 hours — register in the weeks before the auction, not on the day.

TERMS

Lots are sold FOB Panama. Importation is arranged by the buyer, and payment is due within 30 days of the auction closing.
